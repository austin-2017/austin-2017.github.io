V. Discoveries
V. Discoveries We assumed that if followed a checklist according to WCAG and W3C, we would have an accessible site. While true for students who relied on screen-readers, we found it did not necessarily help our students with low-vision and/or motor skills problems, and was particularly problematic for students with multiple disabilities. This also turned out to be financially devastating for both the institution and the student, as the matrix of assistive technology to Operating Systems and browsers was quite large and problematic to keep current. Very importantly, we realized that the Burden is on the student to find the right place to get the right help. We expect them to learn jargon and silos, and then force them to get training to adopt. Solution was to bring content to students rather than have them go get it.
