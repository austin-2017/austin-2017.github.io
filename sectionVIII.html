Fewer steps and body parts involved to speak rather than type


MODAPTS

  1. A methodology used by Industrial Engineers for Ergonometry

    The methodology "quantifies" body movements used in performing a task. There are too many variations involved between a person with disabilities, and one without, to use it to compare metrics. It's comparing apples and oranges. That said, the "lens" by which movements are examined is useful.

  2. Even mouse clicks matter

    There is a functionality all over the web that depends on "double" mouse clicks. There are some disabilities that make it painful or difficult to click the mouse twice, quickly. If the time is too great between the clicks,the computer interprets it as one mouse click, not two. Different operators can adjust the timing by which the second click follows the first, but if the computer belongs to the institution, it's unlikely the student will be able to adjust the speed to accomodate his disability.

Solution: Integrate Voice.

Speaking is easier and quicker than typing.


Solution: Make it work on a phone

Most students already have a phone. If we can get the platform to work on a phone, we may be able to save them the expense of purchasing additional hardware

Ensuring that the platform works on a phone also enables us to take advantage ofthe many voice featuers found on personal phones.


Solution: Build the assistance into the interface as much as possible

Build a special ability to use voicr for students who use desktop computers.

This can alleviate the need by some students to use Dragon Naturally Speaking, or download it, since most students have a phone already.

In the video below, one may see a test of the interface searching for class options for the semester simply by asking for the professor's name.


Screen capture of test of using voice to search for a professor's name in order to search for classes. The name, "Frechette" has nine letters; it takes fewer movements from the body to speak the word "Frechette" than it does to type nine letters. This helps those with motor skills difficulties.
